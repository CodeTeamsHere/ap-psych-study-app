AP Psychology Exam Overview

The course was revised effective Fall 2024; the first exam in the new format was given in May 2025. This site follows that current structure: 5 units, 35 topics, 4 science practices, and a 75-question multiple-choice section. Ignore any older 9-unit / 100-question outline you may find online.

Section structure

Section I — Multiple Choice

  • 75 questions, 90 minutes
  • 66.7% of your score
  • Exactly 4 answer choices (A–D) each
  • No penalty for guessing — answer every question
  • Assesses Science Practices 1–3 only
  • Expect graphs, tables, and figures

Section II — Free Response

  • 2 questions, 70 minutes total
  • 33.3% of your score
  • Q1 — Article Analysis Question (AAQ)
  • Q2 — Evidence-Based Question (EBQ)
  • Assesses Science Practice 4 (Argumentation)

Digital exam delivered in Bluebook. No calculator is provided or needed.

The four science practices

Every multiple-choice question is tagged to one of Practices 1–3. Practice 4 is free-response only.

⚠️ Don't neglect Practice 3

Data Interpretation is only ~10% of the MCQ section, but it's the most under-practiced skill in free review resources. This site includes genuine graph/table/statistics questions — do them.

Free-response formats

Q1 — Article Analysis Question (AAQ)

One summarized peer-reviewed study. 25 minutes (includes ~10 min reading). 7 points across 6 parts:

PartPtsTask
A1Identify the research method used.
B1State an operational (measurable) definition of a variable.
C1Interpret a reported statistic in context.
D1Identify an ethical guideline the researchers applied.
E1Explain the extent to which findings generalize, using participant evidence.
F2Explain how a specific result supports or refutes the hypothesis.

Q2 — Evidence-Based Question (EBQ)

Three summarized peer-reviewed sources on one topic. 45 minutes (includes ~15 min reading). 7 points across 3 parts:

PartPtsTask
A1Propose a defensible claim.
B1 + 2Cite specific evidence from one source (1) and explain/apply a psychological concept (2).
C1 + 2Cite different evidence from a different source (1) and apply a different psychological concept (2).

Cite sources parenthetically — "(Source A)" — or in-line: "According to Source A…". The applied concept must be one explicitly in the AP Psychology CED. The CED and scoring guidelines were updated in October 2025 (clarifications to AAQ Part D and the EBQ).

A simple summer study plan

  1. Read the notes for a topic — focus on the plain-language explanation and the trouble-spot callouts.
  2. Do that topic's practice set until its accuracy chip is green (Familiar+). Read every explanation, even when you're right.
  3. Weight your time toward Unit 2 (Cognition) and Unit 5 (Mental & Physical Health) — they're dense and high-yield.
  4. Every few topics, take a unit assessment to simulate timing and deferred feedback.
  5. Weekly, do one AAQ and one EBQ and self-score against the rubric.
  6. Use "Review my missed questions" to close gaps instead of re-doing what you already know.
