# Sources, Attribution & Copyright

This project is an **original**, free, static study tool for **AP® Psychology**. It is built
against the **current College Board Course and Exam Description (CED)**, which took effect in
**Fall 2024** (first exam administered May 2025). The CED and scoring guidelines were most
recently updated in **October 2025** (clarifications to the AAQ Part D and the EBQ).

> ⚠️ **Do not confuse this with the old framework.** The previous AP Psychology course used
> **9 units** and a **100-question** multiple-choice section. That framework is **obsolete**.
> The current course — used throughout this site — is **5 units, 35 topics, 4 science practices,
> and a 75-question** multiple-choice section.

---

## What is used as ground truth (official & freely available from College Board)

The following are official College Board materials and are used here as factual ground truth.
The **course framework** (unit and topic names, numbering, learning objectives, essential
knowledge, science practices, exclusion statements, and exam format) is quoted or paraphrased
from the CED and attributed to the College Board:

- **AP Psychology Course and Exam Description (CED)** — unit/topic structure, learning
  objectives (LOs), essential knowledge (EK), the four science practices, exclusion statements,
  and exam format.
- **AP Psychology Course-at-a-Glance** — unit weighting bands and pacing.
- **Officially released 2025 Free-Response Questions (AAQ & EBQ, Sets 1 & 2)** and their
  **official Scoring Guidelines** and Chief Reader commentary — used only as **structural models**
  for the format and rubric structure of the practice AAQ/EBQ on this site.

These can be found on **AP Central** (the College Board's official site for educators):
`https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-psychology`

**Everything a student answers on this site is original.** No secure or released College Board
multiple-choice items are reproduced. The two practice AAQ and two practice EBQ sets are
**original analogues** — the studies, sources, data, and prompts were written for this project and
do **not** reproduce any released study text verbatim; only the *format and rubric structure*
follow the released materials.

The following are copyrighted and are **not** copied anywhere in this project. Their pedagogical

- Barron's, The Princeton Review, Kaplan, and UWorld question text and explanations.

All practice questions, notes, and explanations on this site were **written originally** for this
project.

## Inspiration / structure references (not copied)

- **Fiveable** and **Albert.io** — useful structural references for how to break a topic into
  digestible study content.
- **Barron's, Princeton Review, UWorld, Kaplan, and Crash Course Psychology (YouTube)** — general
  content cross-references for accuracy and for the common student misconceptions that good
  distractors are built from.
- *Note:* Khan Academy does **not** currently offer an AP Psychology course, so it is not used as a
  content reference (its mastery-level UX was, however, a useful design model).

## Visual design

The green-based primary color, the conventional green/red correct-incorrect feedback colors, and
the system-UI sans-serif typography are **original design choices** for this project. They are not
claimed to be any other platform's actual brand values (those are not publicly documented).
